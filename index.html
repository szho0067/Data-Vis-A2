Gender Roles in the Workplace

The gender pay gap refers to the disparity in earnings between men and women in the workforce. It is a multifaceted issue influenced by various factors, including discrimination, occupational segregation, and differences in work experience. On average, women tend to earn less than men for similar roles due to biases in hiring and promotion practices, as well as societal norms that undervalue work traditionally performed by women. Moreover, the pay gap is often exacerbated by the lack of access to flexible work arrangement and the expectation that women will take on greater caregiving responsibilities. Hence, women are more likely to work part-time or take career breaks, leading to fewer opportunities for advancement and lower lifetime earnings compared to men.
