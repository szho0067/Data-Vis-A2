Australia Employment

Australia's employment landscape is diverse and continually evolving, with key industries such as healthcare, education, finance, and technology playing major roles. The job market has remained strong, supported by population growth and a focus on skills development through vocational training and higher education. While challenges like economic changes and technology impact the workforce, Australia still offers a range of job opportunities for both local and international workers seeking careers.

Division of Sectors

Industries can be categorized into four key sectors based on their characteristics.

Manufacturing, Construction and Trade, includes: Wholesale Trade, Retail Trade, and Transport, Postal and Warehousing.

Primary Industries includes: Agriculture, Forestry and Fishing, Mining, and Electricity, Gas, Water and Waste Services.

Public Services and Community Support includes: Education and Training, Health Care and Social Assistance, Public Administration and Safety, Accommodation and Food Services, and Arts and Recreation Services.

Professional, Financial and Administrative Services includes: Financial and Insurance Services, Rental, Hiring and Real Estate Services, Administrative and Support Services, Professional, Scientific and Technical Services, and Information Media and Telecommunications.

Gender Roles in the Workplace

Gender roles within the workplace contribute to differing employment rates between men and women. Traditionally, men have been more likely to occupy full-time positions and leadership roles, while women often face barriers such as balancing work with family responsibilities, leading to higher rates of part-time employment or career breaks. How to read the Sankey Diagram

Each flow in the central section represents the transition of individuals from one employment status in the previous month to a different employment status in the current month.

The thickness of the lines reflects the proportion of people moving between employment statuses.
